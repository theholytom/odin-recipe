Ingredients

  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Olive oil
  • Beef
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Remove the steaks from the refrigerator and let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes before cooking. This ensures even cooking.
  2. Season the steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides, pressing the seasoning into the meat.
  3. Allow the steaks to sit with the seasoning for a few minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.
  4. In a large skillet or frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat until it shimmers.
  5. Add the sliced onions to the pan and sauté them until they become soft and caramelized, usually taking about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Once done, transfer the onions to a plate and set aside.
  7. In the same pan, add the seasoned steaks and sear them on one side for about 3-4 minutes until a crust forms.
  8. Flip the steaks and sear the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes for medium-rare doneness.
  9. Adjust the cooking time according to your desired level of doneness.
  10. Add the butter and minced garlic to the pan, and baste the steaks with the melted butter and garlic.
  11. Continue cooking for another minute or two, ensuring the steaks are thoroughly coated with the garlic butter.
  12. Pour the Worcestershire sauce over the steaks, and let it cook for a minute or two, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  13. Remove the steaks from the pan and let them rest on a cutting board for a few minutes before slicing.
  14. Serve the steak slices topped with the sautéed onions and any pan juices for added flavor.
